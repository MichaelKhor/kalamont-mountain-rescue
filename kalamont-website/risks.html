Avalanche Awareness

Kalmont's rugged mountains offer stunning views, but they also come with inherent risks—especially in winter. Avalanche safety is vital for anyone exploring these areas. Here's how to stay safe:

Before You Go:

  • Check Avalanche Reports: Always review the latest avalanche warnings and forecasts for Kalmont before heading out.
  • Understand the Terrain: Familiarize yourself with high-risk areas, such as steep slopes and areas where snow accumulates.

Essential Gear:

  • Avalanche Transceiver: Ensure you and your group carry a transceiver for locating people in case of a burial.
  • Probe & Shovel: These tools are essential for rescuing someone quickly and effectively if an avalanche occurs.

Safety Practices:

  • Buddy System: Never go alone. Always travel with at least one other person.
  • Plan Your Route: Carefully map out your route and avoid risky areas during high avalanche risk periods.

Hypothermia Prevention

Kalmont’s cold temperatures and unpredictable weather can lead to dangerous conditions like hypothermia. Being prepared and knowing how to prevent hypothermia is essential for anyone exploring the mountain. Here’s how to stay safe:

Signs of Hypothermia:

  • Shivering and confusion
  • Slurred speech and clumsiness
  • Fatigue and drowsiness
  • Loss of coordination and consciousness

How to Prevent Hypothermia:

  • Layer Clothing: Wear moisture-wicking, insulating, and waterproof layers to trap warmth and keep dry.
  • Stay Dry: Wet clothing can accelerate heat loss. Avoid getting wet and change out of damp clothing immediately.
  • Keep Moving: Regular physical activity helps maintain body heat, but avoid overexertion.

Safety Practices:

  • Buddy System: Never go alone. Always travel with at least one other person.
  • Plan Your Route: Carefully map out your route and avoid risky areas during high avalanche risk periods.
